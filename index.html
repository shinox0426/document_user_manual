Chapter 1

System Overview

Understand the purpose, core capabilities, and recommended environment for the document tracking platform.

Introduction & Purpose

The system provides a centralized web-based platform for capturing, tracking, and archiving documents so teams can replace manual handoffs with secure digital workflows.

  1. Confirm your organization has a supported browser and stable internet connection.
  2. Gather organization details (address, logo, admins) for onboarding.
  3. List key document types and the teams responsible for approval.

Tips

  • Create a shared folder for templates, SOPs, and supporting files.
  • Decide naming conventions before uploading the first document.

Core Capabilities

  • Centralized repository with rich metadata search.
  • Sequential and parallel approval workflows.
  • Role-based access control and audit logging.
  • Dashboard insights for workflow status and overdue items.

Recommended browsers: Chrome, Firefox, Edge, Safari.

  • Enable pop-ups for PDF previews and export dialogs.
  • Use two-factor authentication if available in your environment.
Chapter 2

Getting Started

Complete the initial registration and secure login process before working with documents.

Organization Registration

Onboard new organizations with a multi-step registration flow that captures company details, administrator data, and subscription preferences.

  1. Enter the legal organization name, address, and primary contact details.
  2. Add the primary administrator account and verify the email address.
  3. Choose the service tier, storage limits, and user count.
  4. Upload any required compliance documents or IDs.
  5. Submit the request and await activation confirmation.

Secure Login & Password Policies

Access the system through a secure portal and follow password rules to protect your account.

  1. Open your organization's login URL in a supported browser.
  2. Enter your registered email and temporary password.
  3. Create a new password using uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols.
  4. Enable multi-factor authentication if prompted.
  5. Log in and confirm your profile settings and notifications.

Tips for first-time users

  • Bookmark the login URL and add the support email to your contacts.
  • Save recovery codes in a secure password manager.
  • Complete your profile photo to help approvers identify requests.
Chapter 3

Command Center

Manage the organization from the dashboard, including subscription and profile settings.

Administrator Dashboard

Use the dashboard as the control center for workflow health, document counts, and quick action shortcuts.

  1. Review the dashboard tiles for incoming, pending, and completed documents.
  2. Open overdue queues to prioritize stalled workflows.
  3. Check storage and license alerts before capacity runs low.

Subscription & Profile Settings

Manage service tiers and user preferences from the account settings area.

  1. Review the current plan, storage usage, and renewal dates.
  2. Upgrade or downgrade tiers based on your workflow volume.
  3. Update profile information, signatures, and notification settings.

Tips for admins

  • Schedule weekly dashboard reviews to spot overdue items early.
  • Export usage reports before budgeting or renewal meetings.
Chapter 4

Identity & Access Management

Define users, teams, and roles so the right people handle the right documents.

Managing Users

Create, update, or suspend users from the user directory while maintaining secure access control.

  1. Invite new users and assign the correct role for their responsibilities.
  2. Verify email activation before granting access to sensitive records.
  3. Suspend or deactivate accounts when personnel change roles or leave.

Creating Functional Teams

Group users into teams such as Finance or Legal so documents can be routed collectively and processed faster.

  1. Define team names that match your organization structure.
  2. Assign members and designate team leads for approvals.
  3. Route documents to teams so anyone can claim and process items.

Roles & RBAC Matrix

Assign granular permissions to roles to protect sensitive documents and enforce the principle of least privilege.

  1. Start with Admin, Encoder, Approver, and Viewer permissions.
  2. Customize access to upload, edit, approve, and archive actions.
  3. Review role assignments quarterly for compliance.

Access management tips

  • Keep a short approval chain for faster turnaround.
  • Enable least-privilege defaults for new users and teams.
Chapter 5

Digital Repository

Organize documents for fast retrieval and accurate long-term storage.

Category Management

Establish a logical taxonomy before upload so documents remain searchable and easy to manage.

  1. Define categories such as Invoices, Contracts, and Records.
  2. Set mandatory metadata fields that match compliance requirements.
  3. Review categories quarterly to keep the taxonomy consistent.

Uploading & OCR Processing

Upload documents easily and let OCR automatically extract text for full searchability.

  1. Open the Documents module and click Create Document.
  2. Upload the file, then enter the title, category, and description.
  3. Confirm OCR language settings and processing options.
  4. Save to generate tracking numbers and QR codes automatically.

Search & Retrieval

Search documents using OCR text, metadata, or document properties for fast retrieval.

  1. Run keyword queries across titles, metadata, and OCR text.
  2. Filter by category, status, date range, or owner.
  3. Open document details directly from search results.

Detailed View & Versioning

Open any document to see its full metadata, history, and version updates in one place.

  1. Preview the document content and metadata side-by-side.
  2. Review version history, revision notes, and approvals.
  3. Track when the document was archived or moved.

Repository tips

  • Standardize titles with dates or project codes for faster search.
  • Attach supporting files (spreadsheets, receipts) during upload.
Chapter 6

Intelligent Workflows

Control document routing through sequential and parallel workflows with clear queue management.

Sequential vs Parallel Routing

  • Sequential: Document flows from one approver to the next.
  • Parallel: Multiple reviewers receive the document simultaneously.
  • Use sequential routing for strict approval chains and parallel routing for faster reviews.
  • Set deadlines based on regulatory or internal SLAs.

Initiating a Workflow

  1. Select the document and choose your routing strategy.
  2. Pick approvers or teams responsible for the next step.
  3. Add notes, deadlines, and any required instructions.
  4. Attach supporting files or policy references.
  5. Start the workflow and track its progress in the queue.

Workflow Dashboards

  • To Receive: Items assigned but not yet acknowledged.
  • Pending: Items currently being reviewed or processed.
  • Completed: Workflow items already finished and archived.

Workflow tips

  • Use parallel routing for routine approvals to avoid delays.
  • Send reminders before deadlines to keep queues moving.
Chapter 7

Tracking & Compliance

Review document status, audit history, and archiving rules for compliance oversight.

Real-Time Tracking

See current document location, assigned reviewers, and timing details in a single timeline.

  1. Monitor which team or user currently holds a document.
  2. Track status changes and timestamps at every step.
  3. Use QR codes to connect physical files with digital records.

Audit Trails & Reporting

Every action is logged so organizations can generate compliance reports and investigate issues quickly.

  1. Log views, edits, approvals, and archival actions.
  2. Generate audit reports for review and compliance checks.
  3. Retrieve historical document movement for accountability.

Compliance tips

  • Schedule monthly audit exports for record retention.
  • Use consistent naming on archived files for easy retrieval.
Chapter 8

Tips & Troubleshooting

Keep daily operations smooth with quick tips, reminders, and fixes for common issues.

Quick Reference

  1. Verify category and metadata before uploading.
  2. Use team routing for faster review cycles.
  3. Keep role assignments current to avoid access issues.
  4. Archive completed workflows at the end of each week.

Troubleshooting

  1. Login problems: clear cache and reset your password.
  2. Upload errors: confirm file type and size, then retry.
  3. Search returns none: broaden your search terms.
  4. Status is incorrect: refresh and review workflow history.
