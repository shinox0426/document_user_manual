Chapter 1

System Overview

Understand the purpose, core capabilities, and recommended environment for the document tracking platform.

Introduction & Purpose

The system provides a centralized web-based platform for capturing, tracking, and archiving documents. It replaces manual processes with secure digital workflows and improves retrieval, compliance, and collaboration.

  • Track documents from upload to archive.
  • Use OCR to make scanned content searchable.
  • Generate QR codes for physical document tracking.

Core Capabilities

  • Centralized repository with rich metadata search.
  • Sequential and parallel approval workflows.
  • Role-based access control and audit logging.
  • Dashboard insights for workflow status and overdue items.

Recommended browsers: Chrome, Firefox, Edge, Safari.

Chapter 2

Getting Started

Complete the initial registration and secure login process before working with documents.

Organization Registration

Onboard new organizations with a multi-step registration flow that captures company details, administrator data, and subscription preferences.

  1. Enter legal organization name, address, and contact details.
  2. Create the primary administrator account.
  3. Choose the service tier that meets your needs.
  4. Submit and wait for activation confirmation.

Secure Login & Password Policies

Access the system through a secure portal and follow password rules to protect your account.

  1. Open your organization's login URL.
  2. Enter your registered email and temporary password.
  3. Set a new secure password with mixed character types.
  4. Log in and confirm your profile settings.

Chapter 4

Identity & Access Management

Define users, teams, and roles so the right people handle the right documents.

Managing Users

Create, update, or suspend users from the user directory while maintaining secure access control.

  • Invite new users with assigned roles.
  • Suspend accounts when personnel change roles or leave.
  • Update user details and permissions as needed.

Creating Functional Teams

Group users into teams such as Finance or Legal so documents can be routed collectively and processed faster.

  • Define team names and member assignments.
  • Route documents to teams rather than individuals.
  • Enable team members to claim and process items.

Roles & RBAC Matrix

Assign granular permissions to roles to protect sensitive documents and enforce the principle of least privilege.

  • Use Admin, Encoder, Approver, and Viewer permissions.
  • Customize access to upload, edit, approve, and archive actions.
  • Review role assignments regularly for compliance.
Chapter 5

Digital Repository

Organize documents for fast retrieval and accurate long-term storage.

Category Management

Establish a logical taxonomy before upload so documents remain searchable and easy to manage.

  • Define categories such as Invoices, Contracts, and Records.
  • Attach metadata that matches business and compliance needs.
  • Keep the repository consistent for audits and retrieval.

Uploading & OCR Processing

Upload documents easily and let OCR automatically extract text for full searchability.

  1. Open the Documents module and click Create Document.
  2. Upload the file, then enter title, category, and description.
  3. Save to generate tracking numbers and QR codes automatically.

Search & Retrieval

Search documents using OCR text, metadata, or document properties for fast retrieval.

  • Run keyword queries across titles and document contents.
  • Filter by category, status, date range, or owner.
  • Open document details directly from search results.

Detailed View & Versioning

Open any document to see its full metadata, history, and version updates in one place.

  • Preview the document content and metadata side-by-side.
  • See version history and revision notes.
  • Track when the document was archived or moved.
Chapter 6

Intelligent Workflows

Control document routing through sequential and parallel workflows with clear queue management.

Sequential vs Parallel Routing

  • Sequential: Document flows from one approver to the next.
  • Parallel: Multiple reviewers receive the document simultaneously.
  • Use sequential routing for strict approval chains and parallel routing for faster reviews.

Initiating a Workflow

  1. Select the document and choose your routing strategy.
  2. Pick approvers or teams responsible for the next step.
  3. Add notes, deadlines, and any required instructions.
  4. Start the workflow and track its progress in the queue.

Workflow Dashboards

  • To Receive: Items assigned but not yet acknowledged.
  • Pending: Items currently being reviewed or processed.
  • Completed: Workflow items already finished and archived.
Chapter 7

Tracking & Compliance

Review document status, audit history, and archiving rules for compliance oversight.

Real-Time Tracking

See current document location, assigned reviewers, and timing details in a single timeline.

  • Monitor which team or user currently holds a document.
  • Track status changes and timestamps at every step.
  • Use QR codes to connect physical files with digital records.

Audit Trails & Reporting

Every action is logged so organizations can generate compliance reports and investigate issues quickly.

  • Log views, edits, approvals, and archival actions.
  • Generate audit reports for review and compliance checks.
  • Retrieve historical document movement for accountability.

Quick Reference

  • Verify category and metadata before uploading.
  • Use team routing for faster review cycles.
  • Keep role assignments current to avoid access issues.

Troubleshooting

  • Login problems: clear cache and reset your password.
  • Upload errors: confirm file type and size, then retry.
  • Search returns none: broaden your search terms.
  • Status is incorrect: refresh and review workflow history.
